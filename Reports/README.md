# Reports

Written deliverables focused on translating technical analysis for non-technical audiences.

## Project: Cryptocurrency Investor Communication

**File:** `DATA_581_Assignment_1_WScott.docx`

### Objective
Translate a technical industry report on cryptocurrency investor sentiment into a narrative accessible to general retail investors. The assignment focused on audience analysis, narrative rewriting, and ethical reflection on simplifying technical content.

### Source Material
**Strategy& (PwC Consulting) Crypto Survey 2025**, a 2,500-respondent survey of retail cryptocurrency investors across the United States, Germany, Turkey, Saudi Arabia, and the United Arab Emirates, conducted January through February 2025. The original report targeted financial services firms studying retail investor behavior.

### Target Audience
General investors with active or emerging interest in cryptocurrency. The audience profile included both current crypto holders and traditional investors considering an allocation. Readers were assumed to be financially literate but not crypto-literate.

### Tools
- Microsoft Word for document production
- Research and synthesis of survey methodology
- Audience analysis framework

### Document Contents
The report contains four sections:

1. **Source Selection (Part 1):** Citation and summary of the PwC Crypto Survey 2025
2. **Audience Profile (Part 2):** A 360-word description of the target audience including their goals, vocabulary, and ethical considerations
3. **Narrative Rewrite (Part 3):** An 870-word translation of the survey findings into general-audience prose, covering portfolio allocations, investor intent, trading strategies, and the 2030 outlook
4. **Ethical Reflection (Part 4):** A 430-word discussion of the challenges in translating selectively-sampled survey data without misleading readers

### Key Themes
- The difference between intent of existing investors and behavior of the general public
- How country-level differences reflect underlying economic conditions (high inflation, regulatory environments) rather than equivalent investment theses
- The risk of "selective enthusiasm" when summarizing surveys of self-selected respondents
- Strategies for honest communication: explicit sampling disclosure, paired upside and downside framing, avoiding language that frames a single survey as predictive
